Interviewer: Before we get into your hiking experience last week, what does hiking generally mean to you?

Participant: I enjoy hiking as a way to do things that are good for me. I like being outdoors, away from the computer and work, to relax and think without constant noise. I enjoy seeing different plants, animals, and views. I also like to paint, so I often take pictures on hikes to paint later.

Interviewer: How do you prepare for your hikes?

Participant: I bring enough food, water, and first aid supplies. I wear layers, use hiking poles, boots, and carry a backpack. I make sure my phone is fully charged and usually bring an extra battery pack for my phone or flashlight just in case.

Interviewer: What benefits do you gain from hiking?

Participant: It helps me get physically fitter, stronger, and improves my cardio health. I enjoy hiking with others because it’s a good time to talk and bond. Mentally, I always feel refreshed, calmer, and ready to tackle other tasks afterward.

Interviewer: When hiking with friends and chatting, does that distract you from feeling refreshed?

Participant: Sometimes I focus more on the conversation and miss some sights, but often the topics aren’t related to my stress, so it still helps me relax. We usually have periods of chatting and periods of silence to take it all in.

Interviewer: How does hiking alone compare to hiking with friends?

Participant: When alone, I tend to think more and notice my surroundings better. Sometimes I go without my phone, other times I listen to podcasts or talks.

Interviewer: How often do you hike?

Participant: Almost every weekend when the weather is nice, usually spring to fall, not in winter.

Interviewer: How long do your hikes usually last?

Participant: Typically two to three hours this year, though in past years I’ve done longer hikes up to six hours or more.

Interviewer: How many hikes do you do annually?

Participant: Probably 20 to 30 hikes a year, mostly on weekends from spring to fall.

Interviewer: Do you hike the same places or different locations?

Interviewer: What motivates you to return to the same hiking spots?

Participant: I enjoy those places a lot, and proximity is a factor since they’re close by. I also like seeing how the hike changes with the seasons and tracking my own progress over time.

Interviewer: Do seasonal changes affect your well-being when hiking?

Interviewer: When hiking alone, how do you manage safety concerns?

Participant: I take precautions by only hiking trails I know from previous group hikes. I avoid new or unfamiliar trails alone and stick to shorter, well-traveled paths where I’m likely to see others. I use an app called Strava to share my GPS location with family so they know where I am and when to expect me back.

Interviewer: Besides Strava, do you use any other apps to connect with nature or stay focused?

Participant: I use my phone camera a lot to capture scenes or plants I want to paint or identify later. I also use a Fitbit to track steps and heart rate.

Participant: No, just my phone and Fitbit.

Interviewer: What is your main purpose for using the camera on hikes?

Interviewer: Do you prefer hiking on established trails or making your own path?
