Interviewer: How do you define hiking?

Participant: There's a broad spectrum, but typically, if I say I'm going on a hike, it's usually at least three miles round trip with some incline, even if not steep. Less than that feels more like a walk. If it's really steep, I also consider that a hike.

Interviewer: In Provo, there are neighborhood trails and mountain trails. Would you classify trail walking as part of hiking?

Participant: Probably not, unless I'm in the mountains and separated from neighborhoods. For example, the Bonneville Shoreline Trail feels more like a trail walk than a hike to me.

Interviewer: How do you prepare for your hikes?

Participant: It depends on the length and difficulty. I always bring lots of water and food, especially salty snacks for electrolyte replenishment if I sweat a lot. I try to get a good night's sleep before harder hikes. Water is the most important thing.

Interviewer: What devices do you usually carry when hiking?

Participant: Typically, just my phone to take pictures or check the trail to make sure I'm going the right way. I don't usually bring a smartwatch or anything else.

Interviewer: What apps do you use when hiking?

Participant: Mostly AllTrails for navigation and the camera app for pictures. Sometimes I check the Weather app if it looks like rain, but that's about it.

Interviewer: Why do you prefer to use just those apps and not more?

Participant: When hiking, I like to disconnect as much as possible. I usually don't text or use social media because I want to be more in nature and not looking at my phone.

Interviewer: What time of day do you prefer to hike?

Participant: I prefer morning hikes around 8 or 9 a.m. It's usually not too hot, the sun isn't directly overhead, and it feels peaceful. I don't like hiking in the evening because it can get dark and dangerous.

Interviewer: Do you feel hiking at night is unsafe alone or in a group?

Interviewer: Do you prefer hiking alone or in a group?

Interviewer: What motivates you to hike?
