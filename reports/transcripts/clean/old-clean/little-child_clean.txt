Interviewer: Can you describe how Jesus began the last year of his mortal life in terms of his relationship with his apostles?

Participant: Jesus intensified the training of his apostles during the last year of his mortal life. He knew that for his message and church to survive, he had to deeply impress his teachings on twelve ordinary men who had only known him for about twenty-four months.

Interviewer: Was there a particular moment that highlighted the apostles' struggles with pride?

Participant: Yes, one day Jesus saw the twelve arguing and asked them what they were disputing. They were embarrassed and silent, but Jesus perceived the pride in their hearts. He then called a little child to him and taught that unless they became humble like children, they would not enter the kingdom of heaven.

Interviewer: How does this teaching relate to earlier scripture?

Participant: Even before Christ, King Benjamin taught that the natural man is an enemy to God unless he becomes humble and submits like a child. This humility is essential for becoming a saint through Christ’s atonement.

Interviewer: Are there any examples that illustrate the innocence and humility of children?

Participant: Yes, there’s a story about a three-year-old biting his five-year-old sister. The father tried to teach forgiveness, saying the boy probably didn’t know what a bite felt like. But the sister soon cried out that he did, showing children’s direct and honest responses. This innocence and humility are what Christ valued.

Interviewer: What qualities do children embody that are important spiritually?

Participant: Children embody purity, innocence, humility, trust, courage, and character. These qualities are precious and can soften even the hardest hearts. Jesus’s tender moments with children, including calling down angels to protect them, highlight their spiritual significance.

Interviewer: How do adults often differ from children in their spiritual approach?

Participant: Adults can become prideful and self-serving, like the Zoramites who prayed with their mouths but were puffed up with worldly vanity. In contrast, children pray with pure hearts, making heaven feel present. Many adults respect Jesus but don’t truly follow or worship Him, unlike children who love Him sincerely.

Interviewer: Can you share a personal example that illustrates humility and faith in a young person?

Participant: Certainly. Easton Darren Jolly, a young boy with Ulrich Congenital Muscular Dystrophy, was ordained a deacon and longed to pass the sacrament. Despite his illness and fear of failure, he insisted on doing it alone. He struggled up three steps to the stand, showing immense courage and faith. His father, the bishop, was moved to tears. Easton’s humility, loyalty, and love exemplify the qualities Jesus taught.

Interviewer: What does Easton’s story teach us about humility and greatness in the kingdom of heaven?

Participant: Easton’s story shows that true greatness comes from humbling oneself like a child. His faith, purity, and determination reflect the highest virtues Jesus praised. It reminds us that children and youth are precious images of God’s kingdom on earth.

Interviewer: Do you have a final testimony related to these teachings?

Participant: Yes, I testify that Joseph Smith’s visions and conversations were real, that Russell M. Nelson is God’s prophet, and that the Book of Mormon is the most rewarding book I’ve read. Priesthood and prayer restore lives, including mine. I bear witness of these truths in the name of Jesus Christ, the humble and faithful Son of God. Amen.
