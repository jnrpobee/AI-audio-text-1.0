Participant: Yes, that makes sense.

Interviewer: Why do you usually go hiking? What motivates you?

Participant: If the goal is to get to a waterfall, my motivation is to get there first. Also, because of my husband—he works in a bank and doesn’t get much physical activity—so I hike for him.

Interviewer: That makes sense. How long do you usually hike?

Participant: Between one and two hours.

Interviewer: Why that duration?

Participant: Because of my knees. If it’s too long, they hurt. Also, I don’t like spending too much time walking; I want results faster. Hiking is good, but if it’s too long, I won’t go.

Interviewer: So it’s both your knees and personal preference?

Participant: Yes, and I think we live in a time where we want things fast. If I get bored, I want to move on.

Interviewer: Which hikes have you done in Utah or elsewhere?

Participant: Near Snow College, around Fairview to close to Mantua. I like it because of the lakes and greenery. Also, Spanish Fork for the hot springs.

Interviewer: Why do you like those places?

Participant: The lakes, the green trees, and the water from snow. Also, the hot springs are a good motivation to reach the end.

Interviewer: Do you have any other concerns when hiking besides your knees?

Participant: No, I don’t really worry about anything.

Interviewer: Do you usually hike with people or alone?

Participant: I don’t like hiking alone.

Interviewer: Why not?

Participant: It’s worrying. I need someone to talk to because talking makes the distance feel shorter.

Interviewer: So you enjoy the social aspect?

Participant: Yes, and having a final goal.

Interviewer: Do you hike for health?

Participant: No, I do other things for health. Hiking isn’t my way to burn calories; I prefer the gym.

Interviewer: What devices do you usually bring hiking?

Participant: Just my phone. I don’t have a smartwatch. My phone counts calories, has my camera, and sometimes I check notifications if there’s service.

Interviewer: How often do you use your phone on a hike, on a scale from 1 to 10?

Participant: About a five. It depends if I’m waiting for an email or text. Otherwise, I mostly use it for pictures.

Interviewer: What apps do you use?

Participant: Camera, video, messenger, messages, email. Before a new hike, I look for info on Instagram.

Interviewer: How do you use Instagram for hiking info?

Participant: I follow an influencer who posts hikes and places. I watch her reels and then go when I have time.

Interviewer: So you get inspired by her posts rather than searching right before the hike?

Participant: Yes, exactly.

Interviewer: How important are messenger and email for you during hikes?

Participant: Messenger and email are mostly work-related. The most important app is my camera because of the views.

Interviewer: You checked notifications during the hike. Did you open every notification you got?

Participant: Yes, when I had service.

Participant: Pictures help me enjoy the experience, but messenger distracts me. Sometimes I miss the view because I’m checking my phone.

Participant: No, I do that for homework but not on hikes.

Interviewer: Why not?

Participant: I think I can do both—check notifications and still enjoy hiking. The distractions don’t replace hiking for me.
