Interviewer: Thank you for your participation in our study. As you know, the main focus is to understand technology use while hiking, including the devices people use, why they use them, and how it affects their interaction with nature. To start, what does hiking mean to you?

Participant: Hiking means going outdoors to get out of a busy schedule and enjoy nature.

Interviewer: How do you prepare for your hikes?

Participant: Before a hike, I find the route, look for pictures online, check the time and distance. I usually take my smartphone, smartwatch, and earbuds. Whether I use earbuds depends on if I’m hiking alone or with someone.

Interviewer: What do you gain from hiking?

Participant: I gain the experience of nature—the mountains, skies, plants—and physical benefits. Mentally and physically, hiking makes me feel fresh and energetic. I love mountains, and hiking helps me return to daily activities with more energy.

Interviewer: How frequently do you hike?

Participant: About every two months, but it’s not a strict schedule. We usually go when we feel exhausted or when friends plan a trip, mostly in summer.

Interviewer: How long do your hikes usually last?

Participant: Typically two to three hours. We avoid very long hikes because we don’t want to feel exhausted; we choose durations that suit our physical ability.

Interviewer: Are there places you like to revisit?

Participant: Yes, we’ve hiked several places multiple times since arriving in the US in January. I especially like the “Y” hike because it’s steep and the view is fascinating.

Interviewer: What difficulty level do you prefer for hikes?

Participant: Medium difficulty. Not too hard, not too easy. My husband isn’t a strong hiker, so we pick trails with a mix of highs and lows.

Interviewer: How do you define a good hiker?

Participant: Good hikers hike often, like weekly or biweekly, have good strength and passion. We have passion but are still building our physical ability.

Interviewer: Why do you prefer to use your phone, watch, and earbuds when hiking?

Participant: The smartphone is necessary for notifications, GPS, navigation, and calls. The smartwatch is mostly for checking the time. Earbuds are mainly for listening to music, but I usually don’t use them when hiking with someone.

Interviewer: What apps do you use while hiking?

Participant: Mostly email apps like Gmail or Outlook, WhatsApp for messages, and Google Maps for navigation.

Interviewer: Why Google Maps?

Participant: We think it gives accurate navigation. We haven’t used other apps like Strava or Trail because the trails we hike are well-known and recommended by others.

Interviewer: How important is Google Maps for your hikes?

Participant: Very important, especially when hiking a new trail. It helps avoid confusion when there are multiple paths.

Interviewer: You mentioned carrying earbuds but not always using them. What do you listen to?

Participant: Mostly music on Spotify. I have a playlist with a mix of English, Hindi, and some drama songs.

Interviewer: Does listening to music distract you from nature?

Participant: No, it depends on the song. If I like the song, it feels refreshing and enhances the experience rather than distracting me.

Interviewer: How do you feel engaging with nature when hiking in a group?

Participant: When hiking with friends, there’s a lot of chatting, so you don’t engage as much with nature. Hiking alone or with just one person is different—you can enjoy nature more.

Interviewer: How often do you hike alone?

Participant: I usually hike with my husband, not alone or in a large group.

Interviewer: When hiking with your husband, how do you engage with nature?

Participant: We talk about the mountains, trees, and compare them to places in Pakistan. It’s a way of engaging with nature through conversation about it.

Interviewer: Do you think about safety while hiking?

Participant: Not really. We feel safe, probably because we hike during daylight and on familiar trails.

Interviewer: During your last hike, how many times did you use your device?

Participant: About eight to ten times, mostly to check notifications, send emails, take pictures, and check navigation.

Interviewer: What was the reason for taking photos during the hike?

Participant: We took a photo of a rock with “Rock Canyon” written on it for memory. Looking back at pictures connects me to nature and makes me excited to return.

Interviewer: Did checking notifications distract you from the hike?

Participant: No, I mostly checked emails because they can be important. I don’t usually check messages while hiking.

Interviewer: How did your husband taking pictures affect your experience?

Participant: I liked it because I didn’t have to take pictures myself. It didn’t negatively affect the experience; capturing beautiful moments is part of enjoying nature.

Interviewer: You didn’t check the time on your devices during the hike. Why?

Participant: My husband kept track of time on his smartwatch, so I didn’t feel the need.

Interviewer: Did recording your device usage during the hike make you self-conscious?

Participant: Not really. We just recorded when we used devices and then continued enjoying the hike. It didn’t impact the experience much.

Interviewer: Why did you choose to hike at that particular place?

Participant: We liked the view and had heard it was beautiful. It was between mountains and lived up to expectations.

Interviewer: Why didn’t you use your earbuds during the hike?

Participant: I usually use them when hiking alone or when I want to listen to music. Since we were hiking together, I didn’t feel the need.

Interviewer: Do you use social media while hiking?

Participant: No, I rarely use social media in general. Using it while hiking would be distracting.

Interviewer: When do you use your earbuds during hikes?

Participant: Mainly for phone calls or listening to voice notes, not continuously.

Interviewer: How does technology affect your hiking experience compared to traditional paper maps?

Participant: It depends on the person. Technology helps with navigation, notifications, and music, especially if hiking alone. It can enhance the experience but might distract if overused.

Interviewer: Do you think safety is important when hiking?

Participant: Yes, especially on trails where there’s a risk of animal attacks or tripping. We usually hike in daylight, so we haven’t been too concerned, but safety should be considered.

Interviewer: You mentioned this last hike was in the evening, but you usually hike during the day. How does time of day affect your experience?

Participant: Morning hikes are better because you can enjoy nature fully. Evening hikes are different and less engaging.
