## Summary

**Long:** The participant grew up hiking and now hikes frequently from Provo, going most summer weekends (roughly 15–20 hikes/year) on outings that usually last four to eight hours and average around eight miles. Motivations are practical (primary form of exercise), psychological (calming and resetting), and social (time with friends/spouse). Technology use is minimalist: a smartphone is always carried and serves as camera, clock, occasional comms, and emergency tool; detailed tracking is typically handled by their spouse via Strava, and they recommend at least one person have a map app like AllTrails for navigation. They encounter no-network areas regularly but are unconcerned because their common uses don’t require data; when alone they might add podcasts or music but otherwise avoid many apps while hiking. Safety practices include sticking to packed trails, using traction devices and poles in winter, carrying water, and notifying someone when hiking solo; they’re aware of smartphone satellite emergency features. The study’s recorder made them slightly more self-aware about checking their phone, and the provided guide app was not used beyond starting and stopping it on this hike.
